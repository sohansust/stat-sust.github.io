Topics of Research Interest

Field 1: Different linear and nonlinear growth models for food grain production can be studied for future prediction. The use of R2, Adj. R2 or RMS as a measure of goodness of fit and therefore as a criterion for choosing the best model is not sufficient. The criteria of randomness and normality of time-series data should be satisfied. This particular aspect of fitting trend and growth rate estimation is rarely given due importance, as a result various misleading inferences are drawn.

 

Field 2: Crime data available from different police stations can be analyzed using generalized linear model approaches. Also hierarchical Bayesian model can be applied and the simulated result from Monte Carlo Markov Chain method can be studied.

 

Field 3: The chi-plot is used to study the existence of randomness in a set of observations. Also the use of residual as diagnostic tool for detecting outliers from an assumed model, and for assessing the overall goodness of fit of the model, is well established. Chi-plot can be used to check the goodness-of-fit of a data to a given model and conclusion drawn from the graphical technique can be compared with those obtained from other established statistical techniques.

 

Field 4: AIDS is getting more concern to the government policy makers as well as to the health workers and as a whole to the society. A large number of studies have already been carried out regarding its awareness. However, modes of transmission such as mother-to-child transmission and heterosexual transmission for such epidemic have not yet been studied in Bangladesh. Stochastic modeling can be used to study such rates of transmission.

 

Field 5: Selecting the best regression sub model or compare two or more models is difficult in two dimensional space. Simulation can be used to derive such a two dimensional geometric representation.

 

Field 6: It is a problem to use fixed and random effects in a mixed linear model. Two kinds of selection problems can be considered. The first is to select the fixed covariates from a set of candidate predictors when the random effects are not subject to selection; the second is to select both the fixed covariates and the random effect factors. Generalized information criteria may be useful but may not work always. Simulation studies can be carried out to overcome these problems.

 

Field 7: Count data show oevrdispersion. To account for such overdispersion alternative additive and multiplicative random effect Poisson model with gamma and inverse Gaussian link function for both univariate and multivariate models can be studied.

 

Field 8: Principal component analysis is mainly applicable to continuous variables while discrete characters can be analyzed with correspondence analysis. Techniques can be developed to simultaneously study both types of data. Also the influential observations and outliers can be studied.

 

Field 9: Univariate normality can be compared according to their power and simplicity, the validity of their reported p-values, their behavior under rounding, the information they provide and their availability in software. Simulation study for small, moderate and large sample sizes and a wide range of symmetric, skewed, contaminated and mixed distributions can be carried out. New test procedure can be derived.

 

Field 10: Low birth weight in our country is a common health related issue. Various parental, psychological, behavioural, social, demographic and economic factors are associated with this issue. Also ultrasound measurement of placental diameter and placental thickness can be useful in predicting low birth weight. Variuos multivariate techniques can be applied.
