Doctor in Philosophy

Admission

     The candidates from Science and Social Science must posses M. Phil or M.S / M.S.S (post 4 year degree courses) degree to admit in the Ph. D course. An M. Phil or M.S / M.S.S (post 4 year degree courses) student may be transferred to the  Ph. D programme after the completion of the first year course of studies on the recommendation of his/her supervisor(s) certifying satisfactory progress of research work and with the approval of Board of Advanced Studies (BAS).

     University teachers with two years of teaching experience and one publication in standard academic journals are eligible to apply for admission. Teachers of the affiliated and constituent college of the universities are eligible to apply for admission if they have three year of teaching experience at Honours level and one publication in standard academic journal. Researchers of recognized research organization with three years of research experience and at least two publications in standard academic journals are also eligible to apply for admission. However, a candidate for direct admission to Ph. D programme has to have First class in Masters or 50% marks in Masters and at least 2nd division on all  public examination.

 

Academic Regulation  

The registration for the Ph. D degree will valid for a period of four years, however the minimum duration for the Ph. D is two years. The registration may be renewed for further period of four years.

Subject of study

          The Graduate Study Committee (GSA) may suggest the required courses for the Ph. D student, the syllabus committee will recommended the courses for approval of the Academic Council through the School and Board of Advanced Studies (BAS). GSC can review the curriculum from time to time and recommended any changes to the syllabus committee as may be considered necessary.

Research Work

         The Ph. D student will perform the research work in the university as a research student under the guidance of supervisor(s) and must submit a report of his/her work to the supervisor(s) at the end of every semester who will transmit the report to the BAS.

         The Ph. D student will give at least one seminar talk at the Discipline / Institute every year on a topic of his/her own field of research.

         The recommendation of the concerned GSC is a prerequisite for ant change of the topic of research. The change of the  topic of research must be approved by the Board of Advanced Studies (BAS) and the Academic Council.

Supervisor

        After the admission, each candidate shall be assigned, by the appropriate GSC and approved by BAS, an advisor or supervisor from among the teachers of the department. The advisor/supervisor shall check and approve student's schedule for the courses, prerequisites, total credit hours as proposed by the GSC recommended by the BAS and approved by the Academic Council. The student is expected to consult his/her supervisor on all academic problems but it is the responsibility of the individual student to see that his schedule confirms to the academic regulations.

For further and full information please contact to the department
