Forced Marriage in Bangladesh- A Study in Sylhet Division 

Mohammad Romel Bhuia

Research Student, Department of Statistics, Shahjalal University of Science & Technology, Sylhet-3114, Bangladesh

 

Abstract

             Forced marriage that helds against consent of either one or both parties, is a pervasive dilemma in Bangladesh and has been recognized as an issue of concern for the community safety partnership very recently. The aim of this study is to explore the status of forced marriage as well as to examine the differentials and determinants of forced marriage in Bangladesh based on a case study of Sylhet division. Moreover through this study an attempt has been made to understand the cultural context of forced marriage and to identify the causes, consequences and preventive measures of forced marriage based on community opinion. This study is based on primary data that was collected using multistage cluster sampling from ten Upazilas of Sylhet division.

The results show that education of the respondents has significant effect on forced marriage. The occurrence of forced marriage was found significantly higher among the people of lower income group. It is found that forced marriage is lower for the people who exposed to mass media at least once in a week. Age of the respondents also have significant effect on forced marriage. Forced marriage was found higher among those whose age at marriage was below average (21years). Prevalence of forced marriage was found highest in Maulvibazar district and lowest in Habiganj district. Higher rate of forced marriage was observed for the member of extended family. The study reveals that marriage choice, selection of spouse in case of arranged marriage and desired time to marry have significant effect on forced marriage. Respondents who desired to take more time to marry and whose spouse was selected by other family elders, were experienced higher rate of forced marriage. Among all the socio-economic, demographic and proximate variables, logistic regression analysis shows that respondent’s education, income, age, selection of spouse in case of arranged marriage and desired time to marry have significant effect on forced marriage.  

According to the community opinions, in Sylhet division prevalence of forced marriage is far above the ground and it is directly linked with dowry, early marriage and various types of enticement. Respondent’s occupation, district of residence, family type, having knowledge about marriage happening by temptation, having knowledge about marriage happening by dowry and having knowledge of suicide due to forced marriage are significantly affecting respondent’s knowledge about the occurrence of forced marriage in their locality. Respondent’s knowledge is also significantly associated with their education, occupation, income, religion, and exposure to mass media.

According to community opinion ‘needs for money’, ‘greediness for getting more money’, ‘social insecurity of young girls’ and ‘for going abroad’ are the major causes of forced marriage. Failure of forced marriage leads to certain crimes in the society such as abduction, acid violence, rape, spoiling the happiness of bride/groom, torture to marry and even compel to marry. According to community observations the consequences of forced marriage are ‘misunderstanding between husband and wife’, ‘divorce’, ‘separation of husband and wife’, ‘tendency of polygamy’ and ‘violence of human rights’. To reduce forced marriage, community people suggested to increase consciousness among the mass people regarding forced marriage, to apply the government law properly. Social protection is also needed and parents should more conscious about future of their children. As a Muslim country, imam of mosque can play an important role for growing awareness to reduce forced marriage. Respondent’s education, occupation, religion, exposure to mass media, district of residence, autonomy to mobility, sanitation system, family type, having any knowledge of forced marriage happening in their locality, respondent’s opinion on ‘do you think forced marriage is a problem?’ and opinion on ‘how society accepts forced marriage?’ are found significantly associated with their opinion on causes, consequences and preventive measures of forced marriage.
