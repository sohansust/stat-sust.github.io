Sumon Kanti Das

Trade Liberalization Impact and Structural Growth of Manufacturing Industries in Bangladesh

 Authors

Sumonkanti Das1,Md. Azizul Baten1, Md. Masud Rana1, and Md. Abdul khaleque2

1Department of Statistics, Shahjalal University of Science & Technology,

Sylhet-3114, Bangladesh

and

2Department of Statistics and Computer, Dhaka Commerce College, Mirpur, Dhaka, Bangladesh

 

Abstract

 

In this paper industrial growth and impact of trade liberalization on it for some manufacturing industries in Bangladesh have been studied by using Census of Manufacturing Industries (CMI) Data for the years 1981-82 to 1999-2000. Higher increasing growth in value added for some industries in the post-liberalization period (1981-82 to 1990-91) while for the most important industries satisfactory growth level in the pre-liberalization period (1991-92 to 1999-2000) has been experienced. Highest growth in fixed capital and improved concentration level in employment have been examined. Comparatively, capital goods industries showed highest significant annual growth rate for value added (13.95%). Almost all industries have shown positive and negative employment elasticities with respect to value added and fixed capital respectively whereas higher positive output elasticities with respect to employment.
