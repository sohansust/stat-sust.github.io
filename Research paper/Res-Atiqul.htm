Impact of Abortion on Reproductive Health and Antenatal Care Services in Bangladesh 

Md. Atiqul Islam

Research Student, Department of Statistics, Shahjalal University of Science & Technology, Sylhet-3114, Bangladesh

 

Abstract

 

In this study an attempt has been made to investigate the differential impact of abortion on reproductive health as well as antenatal care services in Bangladesh. In addition this study also identifies the determinants of induced abortion and antenatal care services respectively.

The study is performed using secondary data taken from the 2004 Bangladesh Demographic and Health Survey (BDHS). The unit of analysis in this study was those women who gave last birth within the 5 years prior to the survey i.e. after 1998.

The study of differentials of induced abortion demonstrates that induced abortion varied significantly for education of respondent, education of husbands, occupation of husbands, place of residence, age of respondents, age at first birth, ideal number of children and complicacy during pregnancy. Induced abortions were found higher among the women who were higher educated, older aged and also lived in urban area. Higher abortion rate has been observed among the women whose husbands received higher educated and engaged in ‘white colour’ occupation. Higher rate of abortion is observed among the women who gave their first birth at the age of 17-19 years. Incidence of abortion was found increasing with the increasing number of problems during pregnancy period. Women who desired small family size (i.e. one child family), the induced abortion rate was found higher among them. A strong significant positive association between respondent’s/joint decision-making score and induced abortion was also observed in this study.

To determine the factors that are responsible for induced abortion in Bangladesh, the logistic regression analysis reveals that education of respondents, husband’s occupation, place of residence, age of the respondents, age at first birth, desire family size, and score of decision-making autonomy have significant influence on induced abortion. The findings indicated that higher educated women were more likely to abort their unwanted pregnancy. The possibility of induced abortion was detected higher for higher score of decision making autonomy power.

In this study, the differentials of receiving antenatal care services (inadequate Vs. adequate) during pregnancy have also been investigated according to background characteristics of the respondents. The result shows that significant inequality in access to antenatal care observed in urban areas as well. The rate of receiving ANC services are lower in rural area may be due to lack of health facilities, lack of adequate knowledge, income differentials etc. The finding shows that higher educated women received more adequate ANC services. That is maternal education increases women’s knowledge about the availability of health care services. Husband’s education and occupation were strongly significant with receiving adequate ANC services. Further, regional variation was observed among women to receive adequate ANC services. Women who maintained media and involved in NGOs activities are more likely to receive adequate ANC services. In this study receiving adequate number of ANC services significantly associated with respondent’s age, age at marriage, age at first birth, number of children ever born, desire family size. The study also shows that women went to take adequate ANC services when they faced problems during pregnancy period. It is reported from the study that decision of receiving adequate number of ANC services is higher in case of respondent’s/joint decision.

Logistic regression analysis reveals that among the background characteristics, education of respondents, occupation of husbands, place and region of residence, mass media, NGOs activities, respondent’s age, age at marriage, age at first birth, number of children ever born, desired family size, complications during pregnancy, and decision-making autonomy power was found significant affect on receiving adequate number of antenatal care services during pregnancy period.
