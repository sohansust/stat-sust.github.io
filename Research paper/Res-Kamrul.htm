BREASTFEEDING IN BANGLADESH 

Mohammad Kamrul Hasan

A Thesis Submitted to the Department of Statistics, Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, Sylhet, as Partial Fulfillment of the Requirements for the Degree of Master of Science in Statistics

 

Abstract

 

Studies on breastfeeding have been a subject of great concern in both developed and developing countries because it has major impact not only on maternal and child heaths but also for reducing fertility. The main purpose of this study “Breastfeeding in Bangladesh” is to describe some existing models on breastfeeding. Behavior, differentials and determinants of breastfeeding have also been studied. The findings of this study are illustrated using the data from a nationally represented survey entitled “Bangladesh Demographic and Health Survey (BDHS) 2004”.

There are six chapters in this dissertation. Chapter-I highlighted the objectives of this study. A brief literature review of the previous works concerning this study has also been provided in this chapter. Chapter-II dealt with the data, methodology and profile of the variables. Chapter-III has discussed the pattern and infant feeding practices in Bangladesh. Chapter-IV concerned with the study of differentials of the duration breastfeeding in Bangladesh for both weaned and censored cases. The determinants of the duration of breastfeeding in Bangladesh have been discussed in chapter-V. Finally, Chapter-VI provided discussion and conclusion of the results and its implications.

 A study of breastfeeding variables such as duration, frequency, intensity etc. were difficult to measure without a controlled experiment involving a large number of women in the reproductive period. Chapter-III is divided into two sections. In the first section, pattern in the duration of breastfeeding had been described by applying various distributions, namely, exponential distribution, modified exponential distribution etc. The suitability of these distributions had also been tested. Second section presents the behavior of breastfeeding in relation to ever breastfeeding baby and percentage of mothers who gave colostrums to their babies, timing of initiation of breast milk and supplementation of breastfeeding. These were discussed in relation to some socio-economic, demographic and cultural characteristics of mother and child by using two way classification techniques. The study further revealed that breastfed children in Bangladesh were given supplemental food early. At the early stage, plain water, solid, semi-solid food, banana or papaya, green vegetables, rice, wheat, meat and fish were the most common supplemental food. Providing sugar water, baby formula and others liquids were not common at the early stage of breastfeeding in the country. Consumption of others liquids declined as children had grown up but the consumption of dal and plain water increased with child ages.

There are two sections in chapter-IV. Distribution of the duration of breastfeeding for different subgroups of explanatory variables was taken under consideration in the first section of this chapter. Different measures of duration of breastfeeding had also been calculated. Second section concerned with the study of survival estimates of duration of breastfeeding. Life Table technique was plucked for searching out the differentials of the duration of breastfeeding of such women according to socio-economic and demographic characteristics of mother and child. This study showed that breastfeeding was almost universal (about 98 percent) and homogeneously prolonged in Bangladesh. The average duration of breastfeeding for last child cohort obtained by Life Table technique was found about 31 months.  Duration of breastfeeding was longer for the mothers who have had longer duration of post-partum amenorrhea (PPA) as compare to the mothers who have had shorter duration of PPA. Older mothers were more likely to breastfed for longer duration of breastfeeding than the younger mother. Urban mothers have had higher duration of breastfeeding than the rural mothers. Mothers of Chittagong division had longer duration of breastfeeding and mothers of Rajshahi division had shorter duration of breastfeeding than the others. The duration of breastfeeding was found shorter to literate mothers than their counterparts. Higher parity mothers had longer duration of breastfeeding than those with lower parity. The mothers whose husband’s were engaged with agriculture and related works and the mothers whose were businessman had longer duration of breastfeeding than were engaged in others profession. Mass media had a negative effect on the duration of breastfeeding. There was no significant variation of sex of child on duration of breastfeeding.

Proportional hazard analysis was carried out in chapter-V to identified the determinants of duration of breastfeeding and asses their specific contribution in explaining breastfeeding based on a set of socio-economic, demographic and cultural characteristics of mother and child. It was identified that the current age of child, administrative division, place of residence, education of mother and occupation of father had played a significant role on the risk of termination of breastfeeding while place of delivery was found insignificant effect on the risk of termination of breastfeeding. Mass media such as television and religion were also found to be significant effect on the termination of breastfeeding.

The author believes that the thesis provides some interesting and valuable results, which may stimulate future research in the area of breastfeeding. The findings of this study may be useful materials for researchers, planners and other social scientists are working in the related area.
